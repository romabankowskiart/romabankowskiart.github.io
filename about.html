Artist Statement

My name is Roma Bankowski. Born in Poland in 1983, I trained at the Academy of Fine Arts in Łódź, where I developed the technical foundations that still shape my practice today. I am now a contemporary abstract artist based in the United Kingdom.

My art is a collection of contemporary abstract works — an expressive exploration of colour and texture. Each piece delves into the depths of human emotion, memory, and shared experience, capturing what is often felt but rarely seen. I believe that abstract art is a bridge between the artist's inner world and the viewer's soul.

My creative journey is a continuous search for the invisible — a translation of internal emotions, fleeting thoughts, and the raw energy of nature into a visual language of texture and colour. For me, abstract art is the ultimate form of honesty.

Each collection represents a different chapter of my internal dialogue. In "The Soul's Mirror", I examine silence and suppression — what we carry that we cannot say. In "Strength & Beauty", I explore the private theatre of feminine experience — women as they are, not as they are seen. "Silents" is a meditation on stillness and solitude, while "Whispers of Nature & Light" moves between the figurative and the abstract, between earth and sky.

Using acrylics, oils and mixed media, I build my canvases layer by layer, much like we build our own experiences. I work with palette knives, brushes, and when the work demands it, my hands. The process is physical, intuitive and deeply personal. Each layer is a decision; each mark is a commitment.

My work has been shown across exhibitions in Poland and the United Kingdom, and featured on international art platforms including Dark Art Movement and Macabre Gallery, where my distinctive abstract style has gained recognition. My piece "Longing for Light" has been particularly well received for its emotional depth.

My mission is to create art that resonates with your personal story. I want my paintings to be more than objects on a wall — I want them to be a source of strength, a whisper of peace, and a mirror to your own beautiful, unseen world. My paintings are meant to be lived with.
